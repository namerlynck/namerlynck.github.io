Personal Info

My name is Nathan Amerlynck and I am 18 years old. Currently, I'm studying computer science at Vives in Kortrijk

Cycling

My cycling journey started in 2020 during lockdown. My brother and dad were already cycling way before 2020. So during lockdown I went on my first rides. Immediately fell in love. At the time I was still playing football. After the 2022 football season I decided to quit and do cycling instead. I made this decision because I liked cycling more. The 2023 road cycling season was my first. During this season I learned the basics of racing but my level wasn't good enough to compete in UCI races. In the smaller local races I could score a place in the top 20 if things went right.

Season 2024

Now onto the next season. During the winter I made a massive progression with my level. Now I was able to "compete" in the UCI races. The highlight of my UCI adventures was a 15th place in the classic region sud in France. Back to Belgium were I also rode to the 22nd place in the legendary "Ronde Van Vlaanderen". Later in the season I went on to get nice results in national races. It started with a 12th place in de "Limburgse Pijl". After that race I had a stellar performance at the "Tryptique Ardennais".

  • Stage 1: 19th
  • Stage 2: 7th
  • Stage 3: 3rd
  • GC: 6th

After that I went on vacation where I took some rest and recovered. Second day back from vacation there is a race in the "Beker van België" in Angreau where I crash in the 2nd lap (out of 10 laps). I had to ride 100km on my spare bike. After not feeling great the whole race I found energy from deep within. I rode to the front and attacked on the second to last time of the climb. Only 2 others could follow. Now in trio we rode to the breakaway that was up the rode. We caught them just before the finish. It was an uphill finish and I was pretty cooked of the chase so I only finished 8th but still a good result. In the final race of the "Beker van België" I was in the break for 80km and still finished in the first group. A very strong race if i say so myself. Then the final UCI race of the year. "La Philippe Giblert". In the end I became 34th out of 150 which isn't good but not bad either on that level. Now the season is over.

2025 will be my first year in the U23 category. I'm hoping everything will go well.
